## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Colombian legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Secretaría Jurídica del Senado (secretariasenado.gov.co)** | Authoritative | Statute text, codes (primary reference) |
| **Diario Oficial** | Authoritative | Official gazette publications |
| **Corte Constitucional (corteconstitucional.gov.co)** | Authoritative | Constitutional decisions |
| **Consejo de Estado (consejodeestado.gov.co)** | Authoritative | Administrative law jurisprudence |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Cross-references, metadata (verify independently) |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases** such as:

- Legis Colombia, Ámbito Jurídico, or institutional repositories maintained by Colombian universities (Uniandes, Externado)

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations (comentarios), and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Colombian law changes continuously through new statutes (leyes), legislative decrees (decretos ley), regulatory decrees (decretos reglamentarios), and court decisions (sentencias)
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Diario Oficial
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position (texto vigente)

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended (modificado) after the last database update
  - Repealed (derogado) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional rules (disposiciones transitorias) affecting its applicability
  - Subject to conditional enforceability (exequibilidad condicionada) by the Corte Constitucional
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** -- Coverage of Corte Suprema de Justicia, Corte Constitucional, Consejo de Estado, and Consejo Superior de la Judicatura decisions is limited; do not rely solely on this for jurisprudence research
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording (texto histórico)
3. **Legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations (comentarios), academic commentary (doctrina), or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Lower court decisions and administrative rulings (actos administrativos) largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of congressional materials (ponencias, debates, exposición de motivos)
6. **Sector regulation** -- Sector-specific rules from superintendencies (Superintendencia Financiera, Supersalud, etc.) may be missing

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary (doctrina) and legislative materials, interpretation may be incorrect
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess obligations under Andean Community law or international treaties
